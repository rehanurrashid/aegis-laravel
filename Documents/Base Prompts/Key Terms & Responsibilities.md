Key Terms & Responsibilities
Practitioner

A Practitioner is a health, mental health, or wellness professional who offers care or services in support of others’ well-being. This term may be inclusive of therapists, physicians, coaches, nutritionists, and other practitioners.

 

Continuity Plan

A structured, written plan that outlines how a practitioner’s practice, client care, records, and responsibilities are managed when a critical moment impacts their ability to practice. It provides clear guidance to designated Continuity Stewards and Support Stewards, including role-specific responsibilities across identified critical moments. Within Aegis, this is called a Continuity Plan and is commonly known as a Professional Will in many professional associations.

 

Critical Moment / Critical Incident

An event that disrupts or prevents a practitioner’s ability to deliver care or manage their practice. This includes traditional scenarios such as short-term or long-term incapacitation and death, as well as modern and complex disruptions such as detainment, natural disasters, missing person situations, and geopolitical or conflict-related events (e.g., war or civil unrest).

 

Support Steward

A trusted individual, often a friend, family member, or close associate, designated by the practitioner to assist in activating the continuity plan during a critical moment. The Support Steward helps initiate communication with appropriate parties, supports the execution of the plan, and may coordinate with the Continuity Steward to ensure alignment. This role is primarily relational and supportive rather than administrative or clinical.

 

Alternate Support Steward

A designated individual, typically a trusted family member or designee, who is authorized to provide support, advocacy, and coordination on behalf of the practitioner when the primary Support Steward is unavailable, ensuring continuity of care and responsiveness to the practitioner’s needs and preferences.

 

Continuity Steward

An individual designated by a practitioner to carry out the responsibilities outlined in the continuity plan during a critical moment. The Continuity Steward supports client transitions by facilitating referrals, coordinating continuity of care, and managing access to records in alignment with applicable standards, and may also oversee aspects of practice operations, transition, or closure. Practitioners may designate more than one Continuity Steward to share responsibilities or ensure continuity if one is unavailable.

 

Alternate Continuity Steward

A designated individual responsible for maintaining continuity of care, services, and decision-making processes in the absence or unavailability of the primary Continuity Steward, ensuring that established plans are upheld and operations remain stable without interruption.

 

Support Continuity Steward

An individual designated to assist the Continuity Steward in executing a practitioner’s continuity plan during a critical moment, supporting shared responsibilities, coordination, and continuity across client care and practice operations.

 

Business Partner

A trusted business or organization that specializes in, or serves, health and well-being practitioners. Business Partners provide essential services and infrastructure that support the effective operation of a practitioner’s practice, including improving operational ease, strengthening service delivery, and supporting administrative, financial, and regulatory compliance.

 

Shadow Practitioner

A practitioner identified in advance through alignment of key factors such as demographics, credentials, services, geographic region, and populations served. Shadow Practitioners are positioned to receive referrals during a critical moment to support continuity of care, and may also accept referrals when a practitioner is at capacity and prospective clients require timely access to services. This role focuses on maintaining access to care through thoughtful matching and continuity support.

 

Integrative Network

A group of aligned practitioners across disciplines that is intentionally established in advance to support coordinated, timely, and holistic client care. Integrative Networks allow practitioners to connect clients to complementary services such as psychiatry, coaching, nutrition, doula support, or functional medicine, ensuring continuity and breadth of care both during routine practice and in times of increased need or disruption.

 

Important Documentation

Additional forms and records that augment and support the continuity plan by providing detailed agreements, role expectations, and specific instructions for the management and operation of the practice during critical events or periods of disruption.

 

Annual Re-Attestation

A yearly confirmation completed by practitioners, Continuity Stewards, and Support Stewards to ensure all information in Aegis remains accurate, current, and accessible, including contact information. This includes reviewing your Continuity Plan, confirming Continuity Steward and Support Steward details, and ensuring alignment with any updates to your practice, services, or responsibilities. It also confirms that roles, responsibilities, and agreements remain aligned since the initial or most recent attestation.

 

Aegis

Aegis is a Practice Continuity Ecosystem that supports continuity and ethical readiness for practitioners. It integrates planning, trusted partners, and aligned resources to manage essential aspects of practice, ensure continuity of care during disruption, and build integrative networks that keep both practitioners and clients connected, supported, and not alone.

 

MA’AT

MA’AT is a strategic partner to practitioners, focused on building structures that make ethical, continuous care possible. It works alongside practitioners to design continuity systems, strengthen practice foundations, and connect them to aligned networks and resources. Through its systems and services, including the Aegis System, MA’AT creates the conditions for care to remain stable, supported, and accessible, so that practitioners and clients are not left navigating disruption alone.

 

Vault

A secure, locked repository where you store sensitive documents and credentials for use during a critical incident, such as login credentials, insurance documents, and practice records. Only accessible after a verified critical incident is activated.
